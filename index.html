Bio

Let’s build together!

My experience working as an Architectural Designer and my love for the design process has sculpted the way I tackle problems. The most direct path is oftentimes not the best path and only through refinement and iteration do we achieve the solution that’s ideal for the user or client. Funnily enough, this iterative mindset closely resembles my own career path, and my passion for creativity and technologies has led me to software engineering.

Having a strong body is just as important as a strong mind. I love to stay active and this is reflected in the hobbies that I partake in. Throughout the weeknights I would be training and instructing in my martial arts. You will find me in the gym on weekend mornings and then on the volleyball court at night. For the more chill hobbies, I also like to go out fishing or spending time with friends playing one of our favourite boardgames (these may not be that chill hahaha).

I often find myself either reading or watching videos late into the night learning about a wide range of topics that interest me. Whether it be watching the latest “Smarter Every Day” video or reading about how to transplant an avocado tree, my curiosity and craving for knowledge is never satisfied. I am immensely eager to learn and will jump at any opportunity to grow personally and professionally.

If my curiosity has ignited yours, feel free to connect and chat!

PokeBattles

The third project of the Software Engineering Immersive Course at General Assembly. This is a team project where the goal is to create a RESTful Single-Page Application(SPA) that will have CREATE, READ, UPDATE, and DELETE(CRUD) functionality. As our very first software development project, this task was the perfect opportunity to experience what it will be like to work collaboratively in the real world.

The theme we have chosen is Pokemon and it will be a smaller version of the classic game. As a user, the goal of the game is to win battles in order to evolve the Pokemon and then have the ability to attain more Pokemon to complete the Pokedex.

Javascript | Express | PostgreSQL | Bcrypt | Photoshop | Canva

Instructor Journal

The goal for this project was to create a Full-stack application and I chose to design mine that has personal meaning and solves a real-world problem. Architectural

A range of different Architectural projects that I worked on during my time as an Architectural Designer.

Australia Post

Since the start of the retail rollout program in 2018, I was part of a small team with heavy involvement in the design and documentation of the stores. These projects allowed me the opportunity to see the complete life cycle from concept through to construction. This included inspecting the site to start the base build in Revit and also checking for defects before practical completion.

Auto & General

Although smaller in scale, this project required careful selection of materials, lighting, and thoughtful consideration of scale and ergonomics. As such, a lot of the documentation was in the details of joinery and working closely with the joiner and coordinating shop drawings.

Medibank

A careful balance between maintaining consistent corporate standards whilst being creative and adding bespoke design elements to each store. These medibank stores were perfect quick design exercises that also allowed for close interactions with the client.

RACQ - HeadQuarters

My main role was to assist in the documentation and working closely with the project leader to provide the best solutions for the client. Weekly construction meetings were held on site and site walkthroughs meant I had the opportunity to see and learn from builders directly.

300 Queen & 825 Ann

My role was to assist the project leader in the conceptual and detailed design stages. Spacial planning and feasabilitiy studies were undertaken to calculate the maximum number of bicycles that can be stored
