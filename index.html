PokeBattles

The third project of the Software Engineering Immersive Course at General Assembly. This is a team project where the goal is to create a RESTful Single-Page Application(SPA) that will have CREATE, READ, UPDATE, and DELETE(CRUD) functionality. As our very first software development project, this task was the perfect opportunity to experience what it will be like to work collaboratively in the real world.

The theme we have chosen is Pokemon and it will be a smaller version of the classic game. As a user, the goal of the game is to win battles in order to evolve the Pokemon and then have the ability to attain more Pokemon to complete the Pokedex.

We learnt so much as a team and I particularly liked how we were able to share our knowledge where others might have gaps. One of the most challenging parts to the project was definitely managing the git flow. I am extremely proud of what my team and I have accomplished and I think it has definitely established a strong connection that will last through each of our careers.

Instructor Journal

The goal for this project was to create a Full-stack application and I chose to design mine that has personal meaning and solves a real-world problem.

The problem:
I am one of many instructors at my martial arts school and the school is spread across multiple locations throughout Brisbane, Australia. The classes are run throughout the week at different locations and communication on what happens at these classes are lacking. A few of the important information that should be kept on record would be what drills/exercises were completed, any new students that might be on trial, and any injuries that occurred during class.

The Solution:
My idea was to create an Instructor Journal where the instructors could share what was done in the classes. This information could then be stored on a database and in the future can be recalled such as searching how many new students the school enrolled in that particular year, or checking whether how a student is feeling after a particular injury.

Conclusions:
In the beginning, I had talked to my Sifu(teacher) about my idea and he loved it. Having a project for a real-world client is an amazing feeling. I cannot believe what was built in just 3 days and with more time, I would love to add additional features and be used consistently by the leadership group. Eventually, it'll have a library of drills and exercises and the planning feature for the app can be created.

Ruby | Sinatra | PostgreSQL | Bcrypt | Photoshop | Canva
